About Me

I am a quantum physicist specializing in optimal quantum control, quantum computing and quantum simulation. I have contributed to major research collaborations, authored multiple peer-reviewed publications, and filed several patent applications. I also teach Advanced Quantum Information at Imperial College London. Originally from Hue, Vietnam, I moved to Singapore for my undergraduate and postgraduate studies before joining the UK's quantum programs.

Research Interest

I am fascinated by the interplay between quantum many-body physics and quantum technologies. My current research focuses on using simulation and optimization techniques in many-body quantum dynamics to address problems in quantum computing, while also exploring how quantum computers can provide deeper insights into complex challenges in many-body physics.

Expertise

  • Quantum control, quantum computing, and quantum optics
  • Trapped ion systems, superconducting qubits, and spin qubits in silicon.
  • Analog quantum simulation of strongly correlated and topological materials.
  • Quantum many-body simulations with tensor networks, Lanczos methods, and GPU-accelerated computing.
  • Programming: Python, MATLAB, Mathematica, Tenpy, QuTiP, Qiskit.

I have written Matlab code for optimal control of quantum many-body dynamics using tensor networks, as well as for exact diagonalization and Hartree-Fock simulation of strongly correlated models. The code is available on GitHub.
